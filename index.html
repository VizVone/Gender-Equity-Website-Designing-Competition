What is Gender Equity?

Gender equity refers to the principle of fairness and justice in relation to the treatment and opportunities available to individuals of different genders. It involves ensuring that all individuals, regardless of their gender, have equal access to resources, rights, and opportunities.

Difference between Gender Equity and Gender Equality

While gender equity and gender equality share similar goals, there is a distinction between the two. Gender equity focuses on addressing the specific needs and challenges faced by different genders, taking into account the historical and social context of inequalities. It recognizes that individuals may require different support systems or interventions to achieve equality. Gender equality, on the other hand, aims for equal rights, opportunities, and treatment for people of all genders without necessarily considering their different starting points or circumstances.

Key aspects of gender equity include:


  1. Gender equity emphasizes the importance of empowering individuals to participate fully in social, economic, and political life. It involves providing platforms and support systems for marginalized genders to express their voices, contribute to decision-making processes, and actively shape policies that affect their lives.

  2. Closing the gender pay gap is also a significant aspect of gender equity. It calls for equal pay for equal work, ensuring that individuals receive fair compensation based on their skills, qualifications, and performance rather than their gender. Achieving pay equity requires addressing systemic biases and implementing policies that promote transparency and fairness in compensation.
